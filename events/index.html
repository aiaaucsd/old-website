Upcoming events

Fall GBM #1

October 10 · 4:00PM-5:00 PM · EBU 2 Rm. 479

We will be having our first GBM of the year where we will provide an overview of what AIAA is, the opportunities available through AIAA, and how to get involved. In addition, three different project teams will be presenting their projects to recruit new members and answer any questions that you might have. This is a great opportunity to get involved and start your aerospace experience at UCSD! The experiences you will gain through AIAA are exactly what real industry recruiters are looking for! The GBM will take place on Tuesday Oct 10th, 4:00PM-5:30pm in EBU 2 Room 584. We hope to see you there!

Learn to Fly with Torrey Pines Gulls Glider Club

October 14 · 1:00PM-4:00PM · Torrey Pines Gliderport

AIAA and Design/Build/Fly will be partnering with the Torrey Pines Gulls Glider Club to teach students the basics of radio-controlled (R/C) gliding. Students will learn about the components that make up an R/C plane and partner with experienced hobbyists to try their hands at piloting. No experience required!

AIAA/DBF GBM #2

October 16 · 5:30PM-6:30PM · EBU 2 Rm. 479

GBM #2 will feature a presentation from UCSD Professor Jan Kleissl of the MAE Department, whose research focuses on solar radiation in the atmosphere and solar power integration into the electric grid through field experimental and computational techniques.

AIAA/DBF GBM #3

November 7 · 3:30PM-4:30PM · EBU 1 Qualcomm Room

GBM #3 will feature a presentation from SDSU professor Asfaw Beyene, whose research focuses on renewables, efficient power sources with emphasis on combined heat and power applications, cycle and energy analyses, mathematical modeling, and simulation.

Tour General atomics

November 9 · 1:30PM-4:30PM ·

AIAA@UCSD will be receiving an exclusive tour of General Atomics' manufacturing facilities. General Atomics focuses on the research and manufacturing of unpiloted surveillance aircraft.

Past Events

Distinguished Lecturer with Alice Bowman

March 8 · 7 PM · Qualcomm Conference Center, Jacobs Hall

Ms. Bowman has worked for over 15 years as the Mission Operations Manager at the Johns Hopkins University Applied Physics Laboratory. In her role, she currently supervises five in-flight spacecraft, one of which includes the New Horizons Spacecraft that passed by Pluto on July 14, 2005.
